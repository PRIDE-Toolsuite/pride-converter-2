  • We recommend that your files are checked thoroughly before submission to PRIDE using PRIDE Inspector. PRIDE Inspector is a desktop application to visualize and perform an initial quality assessment on MS experiments. [PMID:22318026]


  • You can submit your PRIDE XML files containing peptide/protein identifications and your mass spectrometry raw files via PRIDE to the ProteomeXchange consortium. The PX consortium has been set up to provide a single point of submission of MS proteomics data to the main proteomics repositories, and encourage the data exchange between them so that the community may easily benefit. You can download the PX submission tool and we suggest trying out the Complete Submission option, which provides a built-in FTP upload capacity.


  • In case you haven't or couldn't provided protein/peptide identification information in your PRIDE XML files a Complete PX Submission is not possible. You should select the Partial Submission option. You may provide the identification information at a later time to upgrade your submission to "Complete" status.


  • PRIDE XML files are a limited representation of your results, but within a PX submission, you can provide other result or identification files, as well as the raw MS files that can be mapped to your PRIDE XML files to provide as complete a represented as possible. The biggest advantage of doing a PX submission is that a DOI will be assigned to your dataset, providing a stable identifier across all repositories.


  • If you have any questions, please contact the PRIDE team at pride-support@ebi.ac.uk.
